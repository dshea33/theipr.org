Policy 01 · Drug Reform

The RECOVER Framework

"We do not arrest people out of addiction. We recover them out of it — and in doing so, we collapse the market that feeds on them."

Strike One
Warning + Voluntary Offer
Formal notice issued. Brief clinical assessment. Voluntary referral to treatment offered. No criminal record created. Magistrate not yet involved.
Strike Two
Mandatory Assessment + Caution
Formal conditional caution under CJA 2003. Clinical Dependency Assessment mandatory. Treatment strongly encouraged. Capacity assessment ensures fair conditions.
Strike Three
Compulsory 28-Day Programme
Civil health intervention — not criminal. Magistrate authorises within 24hrs. Clinical dependency confirmed in writing. Full reintegration package follows.

Why supply-side enforcement fails
Dealers are replaced within days of arrest. The market has inelastic demand — addicted users do not stop using when their dealer is arrested, they find a new one. The average UK prison place costs £47,000/year; incarcerating a street-level dealer for 2–3 years costs over £100,000 with no lasting impact on availability. A criminal record for drug possession makes employment, housing, and reintegration significantly harder — cycling users back into dependency faster.
Reintegration package (post-discharge)
  • Priority referral to self-contained or supported accommodation with a dedicated Housing Key Worker for 6 months
  • Protection from eviction during the 6-month reintegration period
  • Immediate benefits review; referral to Recovery Employment Partners (guaranteed interview scheme)
  • 6-month paid work experience placement with councils or NHS trusts
  • £500 non-repayable Recovery Reintegration Grant
  • Weekly Recovery Support Worker check-ins (months 1–3), fortnightly (months 4–6)
  • GP registration confirmed before discharge — mandatory
  • Mental health assessment before discharge with onward referral where indicated
Cost modelling — three scenarios
  • 10% uptake (30,000 patients): intervention cost ~£540m vs ~£2.4bn criminal justice cost
  • 25% uptake (75,000 patients): ~£1.35bn vs ~£6bn
  • 40% uptake (120,000 patients): ~£2.16bn vs ~£9.6bn — even with 40% relapse adding ~£480m, still dramatically cheaper
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
Article 5 ECHR has never been applied in UK domestic law in quite this way. The courts will strike this down.
Response
Article 5(1)(e) expressly permits detention of drug addicts. Portugal, Sweden, and Norway all operate compulsory treatment under this provision. The three-strike graduation provides stronger proportionality than most comparable frameworks.
Amendment
Fast-track magistrate authorisation added: within 24 hours of any Strike Three referral, a magistrate signs off the detention on paper — creating an independent judicial record that definitively closes the Article 5 gap.
Objection
How do you distinguish a dependent user from a recreational one at the point of street contact? This creates a risk of discriminatory enforcement.
Amendment
Strike Three cannot activate without a written clinical assessment by a qualified drug worker confirming dependency. The police officer triggers the process; the clinician makes the classification. Annual demographic data on all strikes published by the National Recovery Commissioner.

Policy 02 · Petty Crime

Parallel Three-Strike Model

"A shoplifter with a criminal record and a fine they cannot pay is more likely to shoplift again next week, not less. The system produces the outcome it claims to prevent."

Strike One
Warning + Root Cause Interview
PNC-recorded notice. Brief interview identifies root cause: addiction, debt, mental health, homelessness. Voluntary signposting. No criminal record. Driving offenders: vehicle seized, 30-day compliance window.
Strike Two
Conditional Caution + Community Payback
Formal caution under CJA 2003. 20–40hrs Community Payback. Capacity assessment: if health or addiction prevents participation, structured programme substituted. No criminal record.
Strike Three
Intensive Community Intervention Order
Civil order from Magistrates' Court. 90-day structured programme: 100hrs Community Payback + weekly case management + support programme + housing referral. Force Majeure provision protects against unavoidable breach.
Linked strikes with drug framework
Drug + petty crime strikes count toward same total
Driving offences on separate track
No criminal record at Strike 1 or 2
Force Majeure protects genuine impediment
Full policy detail & opposition scrutiny ▼
How linked strikes work
Drug strikes and petty crime strikes are recorded on the same individual PNC record. Combined totals determine escalation stage. One drug strike + one petty crime strike = Strike Two level intervention. This prevents gaming and reflects the reality that drug dependency and petty crime are usually the same underlying problem. Driving offences (no insurance/tax) use the same administrative infrastructure but do not link strikes with the drug and acquisitive crime framework.
ICIO conditions (Strike Three)
  • Minimum 100 hours Community Payback in 90 days
  • Weekly case management meetings
  • Active participation in an identified support programme (debt, addiction, mental health, or employment)
  • Where homelessness is a factor: immediate referral to supported accommodation
  • Where drug dependency is the primary driver: simultaneous referral into RECOVER framework at appropriate strike level
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
Making ICIO breach a criminal offence criminalises poverty. Someone who misses a check-in because they lost their phone faces a criminal charge.
Amendment
Force Majeure provision added: breach is not actionable where the individual demonstrates a reasonable impediment — illness, caring responsibilities, loss of contact with support worker, or documented housing crisis. Court assesses on the facts, mirroring Domestic Abuse Protection Order provisions.
Objection
No insurance and no road tax are fundamentally different from addiction-driven shoplifting. Linking them undermines the rehabilitative logic.
Amendment
Driving offences moved to a separate parallel enforcement track. Strikes do not link to the drug and acquisitive crime framework. Where a driving offender is independently identified as drug-dependent, they are referred into RECOVER separately.

Policy 03 · High Street

Reviving the Town Centre

"The high street is a public asset masquerading as a private one. Return it to public stewardship and it can be managed in the public interest — not the investor's."

🕗
Late Trading Incentive
Business rates reductions for trading beyond 6pm. Tier 1 (until 8pm): 10%. Tier 2 (9pm): 20%. Tier 3 (10pm+): 30%. Independents and small franchises only. Single self-cert form annually, BID-verified.
🏛️
70% Council Ownership
Local authorities to own 70% of commercial property in designated High Street Zones within 10 years. Rents fixed at 80% of RICS market value — predictable, below market, no turnover gaming risk. 20% of income to High Street Improvement Fund.
⚖️
CPO Reform
Enhanced CPO powers for commercial property vacant for 24+ months. All acquisitions at full RICS market value — fully ECHR A1P1 compliant. Upper Tribunal appeal right preserved. Municipal Property Board with independent oversight.
80%
Of RICS market value — rent cap for council-owned units
3yr
Rent review cycle by independent surveyor
24mo
Vacancy threshold triggering CPO eligibility
Full policy detail & opposition scrutiny ▼
Municipal Property Board — governance
  • Operationally independent of the council cabinet
  • At least two independent commercial property professionals appointed through open competition
  • At least two representatives of local business
  • Acquisition decisions above £500,000 require Board approval and are published in a public register
  • No council may acquire property outside its designated High Street Zone using Municipal Property Fund
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
Thurrock lost £500m. Woking went bankrupt through commercial property speculation. You're proposing the same thing at greater scale.
Response
Thurrock and Woking failed by speculating on assets they didn't understand, outside their local area, for yield. This framework acquires local high street property the council already manages the planning environment for, at below-market rents, generating sustainable income. These are entirely different activities using the same legal entity.
Amendment
Municipal Property Board added with statutory independence from cabinet. Acquisitions above £500k require Board approval and public register entry. No out-of-zone acquisitions permitted under the fund.
Objection
Turnover-linked rents create an immediate incentive to underreport income. Unworkable for council landlords.
Amendment
Turnover-linked rents replaced with 80% of RICS-assessed open market rental value, reviewed every three years by an independent surveyor. Predictable, verifiable, and removes the underreporting incentive entirely.

Policy 04 · Media

Ending the Clickbait Economy

"Freedom of the press is a cornerstone of democracy. The freedom to deliberately mislead the public is not. These are not the same thing."

📰
News Standards Authority
Statutory but editorially independent. Graduated three-tier powers. No prior restraint. Judicial review available. Board via Judicial Appointments Commission-style process — no ministerial appointment.
⚠️
Definition of Misleading Headlines
Five criteria: factual misrepresentation, material omission, false attribution, disproportionate emotional manipulation, sensationalised statistics. Statutory safe harbour for satire, labelled opinion, and commentary.
📊
Graduated Fines
Tier 1 (100k+ users): corrections in equivalent prominence. Tier 2 (500k+): up to £50,000 per finding. Tier 3 (2m+): up to 2% of annual UK revenue. Thresholds on 12-month rolling average.
No prior restraint — ever
Cross-party Parliamentary committee veto on board appointments
No sitting/former minister on board (5yr exclusion)
Annual Statement of Regulatory Priorities
Full policy detail & opposition scrutiny ▼
The five criteria for a misleading headline
  • Factual misrepresentation — asserts as fact something not substantiated by the article or available evidence
  • Material omission — omits information that would materially change a reasonable reader's understanding
  • False attribution — attributes a statement, position, or action to a named individual they did not make or hold
  • Deliberate emotional manipulation — language demonstrably disproportionate to the factual content, assessed against a reasonable reader standard, material enough to cause a significantly incorrect understanding
  • Sensationalised statistics — presenting data technically accurately but in a way demonstrably misleading to a non-specialist reader
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
This is state control of the press. Every proprietor will run front-page editorials calling it censorship the day it's announced.
Response
The NSA has no power of prior restraint — it cannot prevent publication of anything. It can only require correction and impose penalties after a finding. That is weaker than defamation law. A press that systematically misleads the public undermines press freedom by destroying public trust in journalism.
Amendment
NSA section opens with an explicit constitutional statement that press freedom is a foundational democratic value this framework exists to protect. Board appointment through a Judicial Appointments Commission-style process, confirmed by cross-party Parliamentary committee with formal veto. No minister within 5 years may sit on the board.
Objection
A single threshold at 100,000 users creates a cliff edge that will be immediately litigated and gamed.
Amendment
Single threshold replaced with graduated three-tier model: Tier 1 (100k+), Tier 2 (500k+), Tier 3 (2m+). Thresholds assessed on 12-month rolling averages, preventing threshold gaming.

Policy 05 · Social Media

Protecting Children Online

"Children are not a market segment. They are citizens in formation. The state has both the right and the duty to ensure the environment in which they develop is not designed to exploit them."

🔞
Age 14 by Statute
Minimum age for account creation raised to 14 by statute. YouTube explicitly included. Under-14s access only a ring-fenced, algorithmically neutral environment. Document or facial age estimation — not self-declaration. Ofcom sets and reviews the minimum standard annually.
⚙️
Algorithmic Publisher Liability
Platforms are publishers for content recommended to users with no prior relationship with the creator. Technically definable (beyond the social graph). Mandatory quarterly Algorithmic Transparency Reports submitted to Ofcom and published publicly.
👤
Senior Manager Accountability
Named UK-resident Senior Accountability Officer per platform. Criminal liability (max 2 years) for knowing and wilful obstruction of Ofcom investigation only. Negligent non-compliance explicitly excluded.
14
Minimum age — statutory, not self-declared by platform
10%
Of global annual revenue — fine for non-compliant platforms after 18 months
1m+
UK monthly active users — threshold for algorithmic publisher liability
Full policy detail & opposition scrutiny ▼
Why YouTube is included
YouTube's algorithmic recommendation engine, comment sections, and creator economy function identically to social media in their psychological effects on children. The current exemption of video platforms from social media regulation reflects the state of the industry in 2010, not 2026. The exemption is a loophole, not a principled distinction.
The algorithmic boundary — technically defined
Algorithmic publisher liability applies only to content served to users who do not follow, subscribe to, or have previously engaged with the content creator. This is the recommendation engine operating beyond the social graph. It is technically definable, measurable using platform data, and distinct from search results or content from followed accounts. Platforms must provide Ofcom with their technical specification for how this boundary is implemented.
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
Every government since 2017 has announced age verification and failed. VPNs, shared devices, and false IDs make it unenforceable.
Response
This framework does not require perfect enforcement. Liability attaches to the platform's systemic failure to implement adequate systems, not to individual circumvention. A child who lies about their age is not the platform's legal failure. A platform that implements no verification is.
Amendment
Layered enforcement model: platforms implement systems meeting an Ofcom minimum standard reviewed annually. Year One standard: document-based or facial age estimation. Ofcom assesses at system level, not individual user level.

Policy 06 · Defence

National Defence Bonds

"The generation that rebuilt Britain after 1945 did it in part through bonds. The generation facing the threats of the 2020s can do the same."

3-Year Bond
Base Rate + 0.5%
Minimum £50 purchase. Accessible to younger savers. Available via payroll salary sacrifice. Also available as floating rate option at Base + 0.25%.
5-Year Bond
Base Rate + 0.75%
Fixed or floating (Base + 0.25%) rate option. Available through NS&I, all major banks, Post Office, and dedicated online portal from launch.
10-Year Bond
Base Rate + 1.0%
Income Tax exempt up to £500/year within a Defence Bond ISA wrapper. Corporations get 110% Corporation Tax deduction on purchases up to £1m/year.
£5bn
Central scenario — realistic annual raise based on NS&I take-up data
UK only
Citizens and registered businesses only — no overseas investors
CapEx
Ring-fenced for capital expenditure only — equipment, infrastructure, R&D
Full policy detail & opposition scrutiny ▼
Demand modelling — three Treasury scenarios
  • Conservative: £2 billion raised
  • Central: £5 billion raised (≈42% of annual defence capital expenditure of ~£12bn)
  • Optimistic: £10 billion raised
  • NS&I Premium Bonds hold over £120 billion — capturing 3–5% of that appetite for a purpose-linked patriotic product generates £3.6–£6bn
Governance and oversight
  • Proceeds ring-fenced in a National Defence Capital Fund managed by HM Treasury
  • Capital expenditure only — equipment procurement, infrastructure, and R&D; no operational costs
  • Annual public report on how bond proceeds are deployed
  • Oversight by the Defence Select Committee
  • Bonds are non-tradeable — redemption at maturity only; no secondary market speculation
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
Fixing rates over Bank of England base creates fiscal risk if rates rise sharply as they did in 2022–23.
Amendment
Floating rate option added on 3-year and 5-year terms: Base + 0.25% with no lock-in premium — giving HM Treasury a natural hedge against rising rates.
Objection
A 10-year non-tradeable bond excludes younger and less affluent savers. Defeats the democratic spirit of War Bonds.
Amendment
3-year bond added at minimum £50 purchase. Payroll salary sacrifice enabled from launch. Post Office availability mandated. Full range of terms available from day one.

Policy 07 · Business Compliance

High Street Business Licence

"Enforcement is not an attack on enterprise. It is the protection of it. Legitimate businesses cannot compete with those who evade the obligations everyone else bears."

📋
High Street Business Licence (HSBL)
Required for: vape retailers, cash-for-gold, money transfer, nail bars, pawnbroking. Fit and proper person test. Proof of HMRC registration. AML registration where required. Staff age verification training records.
🔍
HMRC Compliance Unit
Dedicated High Street Compliance Unit: 200 officers (Year 1), 400 (Year 3), 600 (Year 5). 36-month inspection cycle for all qualifying businesses. Self-financing from penalties collected. Annual reporting to Treasury Select Committee.
🛡️
Equality Safeguards
Statutory Equality Impact Assessment from Year One. Annual demographic data on all HSBL decisions published. Mandatory review of inspection protocols if disproportionality is identified within 60 days.
First Failure — Age Verification
Compliance Notice + £2,500 Fine
Formal compliance notice issued. Fine payable within 28 days.
Second Failure (within 24 months)
Mandatory HSBL Review
Licence may be suspended or revoked following mandatory review hearing.
Third Failure
HSBL Revoked — 5 Year Prohibition
Automatic revocation. Individual prohibited from holding any HSBL for five years.
Full policy detail & opposition scrutiny ▼
HSBL conditions of issue and continuation
  • Fit and proper person assessment of business owner and any named manager — equivalent to Licensing Act 2003 test for licensed premises
  • Proof of HMRC registration and up-to-date tax compliance
  • A compliant age verification policy where relevant, with staff training records
  • AML registration under the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds Regulations 2017 where required
  • Operating without HSBL where required: immediate prohibition notice and fine up to £20,000
Opposition scrutiny & responses
Objection
The fit and proper person assessment will be discriminatory in practice regardless of neutral drafting. Disproportionate enforcement will face Equality Act 2010 challenges.
Response
Requirements are triggered by the nature of the business activity — cash intensity, age-sensitive products, AML exposure — not by ethnicity or cultural character. A business that accepts card payments and is fully tax-compliant has no obligations under this framework that any equivalent business does not also have.
Amendment
Statutory Equality Impact Assessment from Year One. Annual publication of demographic data on HSBL applications, approvals, compliance notices, and revocations. Where disproportionate enforcement is identified, mandatory review of inspection protocols triggered within 60 days.
Objection
The 70% cash transaction threshold will be gamed within weeks. A self-policed threshold is not a threshold at all.
Amendment
Cash transaction threshold removed as a standalone trigger. HSBL registration required for all businesses in the specified categories regardless of payment mix. HMRC VAT return data used to inform risk-based inspection prioritisation. Businesses not VAT-registered submit a simplified cash flow declaration at application.
Objection
200 officers for the entire national high street is trivially small for tens of thousands of qualifying businesses.
Amendment
200 is a Year One establishment figure. Statutory targets: Year Three — 400 minimum; Year Five — 600. 36-month inspection cycle target for all qualifying businesses. Penalty receipts hypothecated to the unit's budget for the first five years — self-financing expansion.
